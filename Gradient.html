What is a Gradient Descent ?

  • Home

Gradient Descent is an optimization algorithm for finding a local minimum of a differentiable function. Gradient descent is simply used to find the values of a function's parameters (coefficients) that minimize a cost function as far as possible.

How do we achieve this?

We achieve this by taking the derivative of the cost function. The slope of the tangent represents the derivative at that point. We take steps down the graph in the path of steepest descent. Eventually, we reach the minima of the graph.

The learning rate parameter (Î±)determines the size of each step we take along the direction of steepest descent
